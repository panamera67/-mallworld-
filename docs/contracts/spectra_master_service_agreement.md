# 1. Parties and Structure

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") governs the provision and use of the Spectra FX Audit Platform v400 SaaS (the "Services"). Spectra and Client may be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties". The Agreement is composed of:
- This MSA,
- One or more Order Forms (each an "Order Form"),
- The Data Processing Addendum ("DPA"),
- Any applicable Statements of Work ("SOW"),
- Policies or specifications referenced herein.

In the event of conflict, the following order of precedence applies: (a) Order Form, (b) DPA, (c) SOW, (d) this MSA, (e) referenced policies.

# 2. Definitions

- **Authorized User**: An employee, contractor, or representative of Client authorized to access the Services.
- **Client Data**: Data uploaded or otherwise provided to Spectra by or on behalf of Client, including anonymized FX transaction data and associated metadata.
- **Confidential Information**: Non-public information disclosed by a Party that is marked or understood to be confidential.
- **Documentation**: Spectra's technical and user documentation describing the Services.
- **Platform**: Spectra FX Audit Platform v400, including APIs, web interfaces, ML modules, and observability dashboards.
- **Report**: Analytics outputs generated by the Platform (executive summaries, recommendations, dashboards, exports).
- **Subscription Term**: The period defined in an Order Form during which Client is entitled to access the Services.

# 3. Services and Access Rights

3.1 **License**. Subject to timely payment of fees, Spectra grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable, limited right to access and use the Platform during the Subscription Term for Client's internal business purposes and in accordance with this Agreement.

3.2 **Account Provisioning**. Spectra will provision tenant-isolated environments. Client will designate an administrator to manage Authorized Users and roles (admin, analyst, client, viewer). Client is responsible for its users' compliance.

3.3 **Service Levels**. Spectra will make commercially reasonable efforts to maintain 99.5% monthly uptime for the Platform. Planned maintenance windows will be communicated at least 48 hours in advance.

3.4 **Support**. Standard support includes email support during business hours (CET) with a 1-business-day response SLA. Enterprise support (if purchased) includes 24/7 coverage with response within 4 hours.

# 4. Implementation and Professional Services

4.1 **Onboarding**. Spectra will assist with initial onboarding, including secure upload setup, role mapping, and report configuration as described in the Order Form.

4.2 **Professional Services**. Additional consulting, integration, or custom development services will be set out in SOWs referencing this Agreement. Unless specified otherwise, deliverables from Professional Services will be deemed "Reports".

# 5. Security and Data Protection

5.1 **Security Controls**. Spectra will maintain industry-standard security safeguards, including encrypted data transfer (TLS 1.2+), tenant-isolated storage, role-based access, continuous monitoring, and log retention consistent with ISO 27001/FS standards.

5.2 **Data Retention**. Spectra retains Client Data for the duration of the Subscription Term plus 30 days unless otherwise agreed. Client may request accelerated deletion via support channels.

5.3 **Incident Response**. Spectra will notify Client without undue delay after becoming aware of a security incident affecting Client Data. Notifications will include known impact, remediation steps, and contact information.

5.4 **Compliance**. Spectra will process Client Data in accordance with the DPA and applicable data protection laws, including GDPR when relevant.

# 6. Client Responsibilities

6.1 **Legal Rights**. Client represents that it has obtained all consents, rights, and authorizations necessary to provide Client Data and to permit Spectra to perform the Services.

6.2 **Security Cooperation**. Client will implement reasonable safeguards to protect account credentials and will promptly notify Spectra of any suspected unauthorized access.

6.3 **Usage Restrictions**. Client will not (a) reverse engineer the Platform, (b) access the Platform for competitive benchmarking, (c) use the Services to process unlawful data, or (d) introduce malicious code.

# 7. Fees, Billing, and Taxes

7.1 **Fees**. Fees are specified in the applicable Order Form and may include subscription tiers (Free, Standard, Enterprise), usage-based charges, and Professional Services fees.

7.2 **Billing**. Unless otherwise stated, subscription fees are invoiced annually in advance. Usage-based fees are invoiced monthly in arrears. Invoices are due within 30 days of issuance.

7.3 **Late Payment**. Overdue amounts accrue interest at 1.5% per month (or the maximum permitted by law, if lower). Spectra may suspend Services for payments overdue more than 30 days after notice.

7.4 **Taxes**. Fees exclude taxes. Client is responsible for applicable VAT, sales, or similar taxes, except for taxes on Spectra's income.

# 8. Intellectual Property

8.1 **Spectra IP**. Spectra retains all rights, title, and interest in the Platform, Documentation, and underlying technology. No rights are granted except as expressly stated.

8.2 **Client Data**. Client retains ownership of Client Data. Spectra may use Client Data solely to provide the Services, maintain security, produce Reports, and enhance Platform features in a de-identified aggregate form.

8.3 **Feedback**. Client grants Spectra a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use suggestions or feedback provided by Client to improve the Services.

# 9. Confidentiality

9.1 **Obligations**. The receiving Party will use the disclosing Party's Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement, will apply at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, and will restrict access to personnel or contractors with a need to know and subject to confidentiality obligations.

9.2 **Exclusions**. Confidential Information excludes information that (i) is already public, (ii) was known to the receiving Party without confidentiality obligations, (iii) is independently developed, or (iv) is disclosed under legal compulsion (with prompt notice to the disclosing Party).

9.3 **Return/Destruction**. Upon termination, each Party will return or destroy the other's Confidential Information upon request, except as required by law or internal compliance policies.

# 10. Representations and Warranties

10.1 **Mutual**. Each Party represents that it has the legal authority to enter into this Agreement.

10.2 **Spectra**. Spectra warrants that the Services will materially conform to the Documentation and that Professional Services will be performed in a professional manner consistent with industry standards.

10.3 **Client**. Client warrants that Client Data does not infringe third-party rights and that Client will use the Services in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

10.4 **Disclaimer**. Except as expressly stated, the Services are provided "as is" without warranties of any kind, including implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement.

# 11. Indemnification

11.1 **By Spectra**. Spectra will defend and indemnify Client against third-party claims alleging that the Services infringe intellectual property rights, provided Client promptly notifies Spectra and cooperates at Spectra's expense.

11.2 **By Client**. Client will defend and indemnify Spectra against claims arising from (i) Client Data, (ii) Client's breach of this Agreement, or (iii) Client's use of the Services in violation of law.

11.3 **Remedies**. In response to an infringement claim, Spectra may (a) modify the Services, (b) procure necessary rights, or (c) terminate the affected Services with pro-rated refunds.

# 12. Limitation of Liability

12.1 **Cap**. Except for Excluded Claims, each Party's aggregate liability under this Agreement will not exceed the fees paid or payable by Client to Spectra in the 12 months preceding the claim.

12.2 **Excluded Claims**. Limits do not apply to (a) Client's payment obligations, (b) either Party's indemnification obligations, (c) breach of confidentiality, or (d) damages arising from gross negligence or willful misconduct.

12.3 **Consequential Damages**. Neither Party will be liable for indirect, incidental, punitive, or consequential damages, including lost profits or loss of data, even if advised of the possibility.

# 13. Term and Termination

13.1 **Term**. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated as provided herein.

13.2 **Termination for Convenience**. Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any Order Form for convenience by providing 90 days' written notice, unless the Order Form specifies a committed term.

13.3 **Termination for Cause**. Either Party may terminate upon 30 days' written notice if the other Party materially breaches and fails to cure within the notice period. Immediate termination is permitted for insolvency events.

13.4 **Effect of Termination**. Upon termination, Client's access to the Services will cease. Spectra will provide Client, upon request within 30 days, with a final export of Client Data in a mutually agreed format. Outstanding fees remain due.

# 14. Audit and Compliance

14.1 **Audit Rights**. During the Subscription Term, Spectra will provide reasonable responses to Client's audit requests concerning security and compliance, including SOC 2 reports or equivalent. On-site audits are subject to prior written agreement and may be billed as Professional Services.

14.2 **Regulatory Cooperation**. Spectra will cooperate with regulatory inquiries related to the Services, provided Client reimburses reasonable costs associated with bespoke reporting.

# 15. Publicity

Spectra may list Client's name and logo on Spectra's website and marketing materials unless Client opts out in writing. Press releases require mutual written consent.

# 16. Miscellaneous

16.1 **Independent Contractors**. The Parties are independent contractors; neither Party is the agent or partner of the other.

16.2 **Assignment**. Client may not assign this Agreement without Spectra's prior written consent, except to an affiliate or in connection with a merger or acquisition. Any prohibited assignment is void.

16.3 **Governing Law**. This Agreement is governed by the laws of France (excluding conflict of laws principles). The courts of Paris, France have exclusive jurisdiction, except Spectra may seek injunctive relief in any jurisdiction.

16.4 **Notices**. Notices will be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation) or registered mail to the addresses specified in the Order Form.

16.5 **Entire Agreement**. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes prior agreements regarding its subject matter. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

16.6 **Severability and Waiver**. If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect. Failure to enforce any provision shall not be deemed a waiver.
